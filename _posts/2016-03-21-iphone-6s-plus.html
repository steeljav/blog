After ~7 years of being an android phone user I bought an iPhone 6S Plus and I couldn't be happier with it. I did have an iPod touch that I used for about a year in between somewhere.

I needed to get a new phone anyway and decided on either the google Nexus or the iPhone. The main reason I decided against the Nexus was that I was tired of the Android weirdness. This may not have been an issue with the Nexus, but it has been an issue with every other Android phone I have had. By Android weirdness I mean strange things happening on the phone like it randomly heating up for no reason, rebooting for no reason, etc. A few other things about Android bothered me:

  1. Very few droid phones still have micro SD slots. I saw that as a major advantage over the iPhone. I have no idea why manufacturers quit including it.
  2. All of the manufacturers (HTC, LG, Samsung, etc.) put their bloatware on their droid phones and you can not get rid of it.
  3. If you get a droid phone from your carrier it will have their bloatware on it and you can not get rid of it.
  4. I was tired of dealing with apps that only work partially on the Android platform.

There are probably a few other things I'm forgetting about but the main thing was that I wanted an unlocked phone that didn't have bloatware, wouldn't randomly do unexplainable weird things and that all apps would work on. The iPhone meets all of those requirements.
